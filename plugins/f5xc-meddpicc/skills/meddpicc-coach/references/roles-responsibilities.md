# MEDDPICC Roles & Responsibilities

RACI-style ownership by role. Every MEDDPICC element has a clear
owner — shared accountability leads to no accountability.

R = Responsible, A = Accountable, C = Consulted, I = Informed

---

## Account Executive / Account Manager (AE/AM)

**Primary accountability:** Revenue outcome and overall MEDDPICC
integrity.

**Key responsibilities:**

- Own the MEDDPICC record and keep it evidence-based
- Secure Economic Buyer access, run executive alignment, manage politics
- Build and govern the Mutual Action Plan
- Own commercial strategy: packaging, pricing, negotiation, competitive
  positioning
- Drive Paper Process to signature and ensure forecast integrity

**Common AE pitfalls:**

- Delegating EB engagement to others
- Calling a "supporter" a champion without proof
- Letting POV run without exit criteria
- Updating MEDDPICC only during QBRs instead of continuously

---

## Sales Engineer / Solutions Engineer (SE)

**Primary accountability:** Technical win, risk elimination, proof
artifacts.

**Key responsibilities:**

- Lead technical discovery; translate pain into technical requirements
  tied to metrics
- Shape Decision Criteria in your favor via architecture and
  differentiation
- Design POV/pilot success criteria; execute proof and document results
- Own technical stakeholder mapping (security, platform, network, app
  teams)
- Produce: reference architecture, validation report, implementation
  plan outline
- Support security questionnaires, compliance mapping, and technical
  objections

**SE operating rule:** If you can't tie the technical solution to
Metrics + Decision Criteria, you're doing activity — not progress.

---

## Customer Success Manager (CSM)

**Primary accountability:** Value realization, adoption credibility,
expansion drivers.

**Key responsibilities:**

- Validate current-state outcomes and gaps (especially in existing
  accounts)
- Provide internal references: adoption metrics, success stories, risk
  flags
- Co-author success plan aligned to the buyer's Metrics
- Identify renewal/expansion timing and stakeholders

**When CSM is critical:** Expansions, renewals, competitive
displacement, "why change" urgency.

---

## Professional Services / Solutions Delivery

**Primary accountability:** Feasible deployment plan and
time-to-value.

**Key responsibilities:**

- Confirm implementation scope, timeline, and prerequisites
- Reduce delivery risk in regulated or complex environments
- Provide SOW, project plan, and resourcing model when needed

---

## Product / Engineering Specialists

**Primary accountability:** Credibility on roadmap, deep technical
edge cases.

**Key responsibilities:**

- Join only when there is a clear "why now" (blocker, differentiation,
  roadmap dependency)
- Provide authoritative answers; avoid "science projects"
- Support competitive differentiators with proof points

---

## Channel / Partner Manager

**Primary accountability:** Partner alignment and influence leverage.

**Key responsibilities:**

- Define co-sell roles: who leads, who delivers, who bills
- Use partner relationships for EB access or procurement acceleration
- Ensure partner commitments align to MAP

---

## Deal Desk / RevOps

**Primary accountability:** Commercial accuracy and policy compliance.

**Key responsibilities:**

- Quote structure, approvals, discount governance
- Ensure terms align to internal rules and margin targets
- Support forecasting hygiene (stage definitions, MEDDPICC standards)

---

## Legal, InfoSec, Finance (Internal)

**Primary accountability:** Risk management and contractual readiness.

**Key responsibilities:**

- Legal: redlines, DPA/SLA review, negotiation support
- InfoSec: security posture, compliance evidence, customer
  questionnaires
- Finance: payment terms, revenue recognition constraints
