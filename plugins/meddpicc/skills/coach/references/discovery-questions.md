# MEDDPICC Discovery Questions

Field-tested questions to strengthen each MEDDPICC element. Use these
throughout the deal cycle — not just in initial discovery.

---

## Metrics

**Establishing baseline and targets:**

- "What does success look like in numbers? What baseline are you at today?"
- "If we delivered that improvement, who gets credit?"
- "How do you measure that today? What system captures the data?"
- "What's the financial impact of a 1% improvement in [metric]?"
- "What targets has your leadership set for this initiative?"

**Connecting metrics to budget:**

- "If we could prove [X] improvement, would that justify the investment?"
- "How does this initiative tie to your organization's OKRs or KPIs?"
- "What would it mean for your team if you hit these numbers?"

---

## Economic Buyer

**Identifying the EB:**

- "Who owns the budget for this initiative?"
- "If we can prove value, who needs to approve the funding?"
- "Who has the authority to reallocate budget if needed?"
- "Walk me through how budget decisions work for initiatives like this"
- "Is this funded from an existing budget line or does it need new approval?"

**Engaging the EB:**

- "Would it be helpful to schedule a 30-minute executive briefing?"
- "What does [EB name] care most about right now?"
- "What would make [EB name] a hero internally if this succeeds?"
- "What risk does [EB name] face if this doesn't get solved?"

---

## Decision Criteria

**Uncovering criteria:**

- "What are the top 3 requirements you'll use to select a solution?"
- "What would disqualify a vendor?"
- "How are you weighting technical vs. business vs. commercial criteria?"
- "Are there any must have requirements that are non-negotiable?"
- "Who defined these criteria, and are all stakeholders aligned?"

**Influencing criteria:**

- "Based on what we've discussed, would [capability] be important to evaluate?"
- "Other customers in your industry have found [X] to be critical — is
  that on your radar?"
- "How important is [your differentiator] compared to [commodity feature]?"

---

## Decision Process

**Mapping the process:**

- "Walk me through the steps from 'yes, we want this' to signed agreement"
- "Who is involved at each step, and what do they need to approve?"
- "What's happened in the past when your organization bought something
  like this?"
- "Are there any upcoming events that could accelerate or delay the
  timeline?"
- "Is there a committee or board that needs to review?"

**Validating the process:**

- "Has this process changed recently?"
- "Are there any steps we should start in parallel to save time?"
- "What typically causes delays in your approval process?"

---

## Paper Process

**Discovering procurement mechanics:**

- "What does procurement need from us, and how long does each step take?"
- "Do you have a vendor onboarding portal we should register with?"
- "What security artifacts do you typically require (SOC2, pen test, DPIA)?"
- "Who handles legal review, and what's their typical turnaround?"
- "Are there standard contract terms we should review early?"

**Accelerating paper process:**

- "Can we start the security questionnaire now while the evaluation
  continues?"
- "Would it help to share our standard legal terms for early review?"
- "What format does your team need for quotes and proposals?"

---

## Identify Pain

**Uncovering pain:**

- "What happens if this doesn't change in the next 90 days?"
- "What's the triggering event that made this urgent now?"
- "How is this problem affecting your team day-to-day?"
- "What have you tried before, and why didn't it work?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would be different tomorrow?"

**Quantifying pain:**

- "What's the cost of this problem — in dollars, hours, or risk?"
- "How many people are affected by this issue?"
- "What's the worst-case scenario if this isn't addressed?"
- "How does this pain compare to your other priorities?"

**Testing urgency:**

- "Is this a 'must solve now' or a 'nice to solve eventually'?"
- "What's driving the timeline — is there an external deadline?"
- "Who else in your organization feels this pain?"

---

## Champion

**Identifying a champion:**

- "Who internally is most passionate about solving this problem?"
- "If you had to sell this internally, what objections will come up?"
- "Who would benefit most if this initiative succeeds?"
- "Is there someone who has driven similar changes before?"

**Testing champion strength:**

- "Can we align on a 30-minute session with your executive sponsor?"
- "Would you be willing to present our value proposition to [EB]?"
- "What's the internal narrative you'd use to advocate for this?"
- "What objections do you expect, and how would you handle them?"
- "Can you share the internal evaluation scorecard with us?"

**Coaching the champion:**

- "What ammunition do you need from us to make the case internally?"
- "What would make your life easier as you navigate this internally?"
- "How can we help you look good in front of your leadership?"

---

## Competition

**Identifying competition:**

- "Who else are you considering, and what do you like about them?"
- "Is 'do nothing' or 'build internally' a real option?"
- "Have you used any of these vendors before?"
- "What's your current solution, and what's driving the change?"

**Understanding competitive position:**

- "What do you see as the key differences between the options?"
- "Where do you think we're strong, and where do you have concerns?"
- "What would the other vendor need to do to win your business?"
- "If you had to decide today, which direction would you lean?"

**Competitive differentiation:**

- "Can I show you how we handle [competitive weakness area] differently?"
- "Would a head-to-head proof on [key criterion] be valuable?"
- "Other customers who evaluated [competitor] chose us because [X] —
  would that be relevant for you?"
