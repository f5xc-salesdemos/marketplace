# MEDDPICC by Deal Stage

What must be true before a deal advances. Use these stage-gate
criteria to prevent premature advancement and forecast inflation.

---

## Stage 1 — Early Qualification

**Goal:** Confirm the deal is real and worth pursuing.

**Must be true:**

- [ ] Pain is real, current, and agreed by at least one stakeholder
- [ ] Initial Metrics hypothesis exists (baseline + target)
- [ ] Competition is identified (vendors, build, do-nothing)
- [ ] Decision Criteria and Decision Process are partially known
- [ ] ICP fit confirmed (industry, size, use case, margin)

**Key outputs:**

- Draft pain statement (impact + urgency)
- Stakeholder map (EB candidates, champion candidates, blockers)
- Competitive landscape hypothesis
- First-pass decision/paper process outline

**Owners:**

- AE: Deal hypothesis and stakeholder access plan
- SE: Technical problem framing and proof path hypothesis
- CSM: Adoption baseline and expansion drivers (existing accounts)
- Sales Leader: Confirms deal is worth pursuing

---

## Stage 2 — Solution Validation / POV

**Goal:** Replace assumptions with validated evidence.

**Must be true:**

- [ ] POV/pilot success criteria written and approved by customer
- [ ] Champion identified and actively taking actions
- [ ] Decision Criteria ranked and influenced toward your strengths
- [ ] Decision Process mapped with dates and gate owners
- [ ] Metrics baseline confirmed and target agreed
- [ ] Pain quantified with consequence of inaction

**Key outputs:**

- Written success criteria (signed off before POV kickoff)
- Short list of tests aligned to ranked Decision Criteria
- Clear differentiation tests (prove what competitors can't)
- Early identification of implementation and security hurdles

**Non-negotiables for technical proof:**

- Written success criteria signed off by customer before kickoff
- Tests aligned to ranked Decision Criteria
- Differentiation tests planned
- Exit criteria defined (POV does not run indefinitely)

**Owners:**

- SE: Proof design, execution, and documentation
- AE: Executive visibility and MAP governance

---

## Stage 3 — Proposal / Commit

**Goal:** Secure EB sponsorship and make the purchase predictable.

**Must be true:**

- [ ] Economic Buyer identified, engaged, and sponsoring
- [ ] Business case validated with customer-owned Metrics
- [ ] Paper Process verified with named owners and timeline
- [ ] Competitive strategy clear and defensible
- [ ] Mutual Action Plan dates intact and buyer-confirmed
- [ ] Champion tested (concrete action taken this week)

**Key outputs:**

- Business case: baseline → target metrics, value model, cost of delay
- Commercial structure aligned to value realization
- Architecture summary and technical risk mitigation
- Adoption and success plan

**Owners:**

- AE: Executive narrative, pricing strategy, closing plan
- SE: Technical risk mitigation, architecture summary, proof artifacts
- CSM/PS: Adoption plan, success milestones, references

---

## Stage 4 — Negotiation / Paper

**Goal:** Make purchase mechanics predictable and fast.

**Must be true:**

- [ ] Procurement steps documented with owners and timelines
- [ ] Security review artifacts submitted
- [ ] Legal terms in review or agreed
- [ ] Quote format and PO requirements met
- [ ] Signature workflow and approval chain confirmed

**Owners:**

- AE: Paper process timeline and escalation
- Deal Desk/RevOps: Quote accuracy, approvals, policy compliance
- Legal: Redline review
- SE: Security questionnaires and technical compliance

---

## Stage 5 — Closed Won (Handoff Readiness)

**Goal:** Ensure successful transition to delivery and adoption.

**Must be true:**

- [ ] Implementation plan agreed with customer
- [ ] Success plan aligned to Metrics
- [ ] Executive sponsors confirmed for adoption checkpoints
- [ ] CSM/PS introduced and briefed on deal context
- [ ] Renewal/expansion triggers identified

**Forecast rule of thumb:** A deal is not "Commit" unless EB, MAP
dates, and Paper Process are verified. A deal with gaps in these
three elements is "Best Case" at most.
